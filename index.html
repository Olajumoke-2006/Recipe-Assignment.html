Jollof Rice & Chicken

A rich, smoky and spicy West African favorite: Perfectly cooked jollof rice paired with tender, flavorful chicken.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 45 minutes
  • Servings: 6
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

For the Jollof Rice

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cups chicken stock or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Chicken

  • 6 chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, or breasts)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chicken: Pat chicken dry with paper towels. Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder and ginger.
  2. In a large pot, heat ¼ cup of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onion and sauté.
  3. Add the seasoned chicken pieces to the pan and brown on all sides (about 5–7 minutes per side). Once browned, remove the chicken and set aside.
  4. Make the base sauce for the rice: In the same pan (or a separate large, deep pot), add a little more oil if needed. Sauté the chopped onion until soft, then add the minced garlic, chopped tomatoes, and red bell pepper.
  5. Add the tomato paste, stirring frequently to prevent burning. Let it fry for a few minutes until the paste becomes sweet and fragrant.
  6. Season the sauce with thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Stir thoroughly so all the flavors combine.
  7. Add the rice to the sauce, stirring to coat every grain in the tomato-pepper mixture.
  8. Pour in the chicken stock (or water) and add the bay leaf. Stir gently, bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  9. Cover the pot and cook the rice for about 20–25 minutes, checking occasionally to ensure it’s not burning. If needed, add a little more stock/water.
  10. While the rice is cooking, return the browned chicken to the pot, adding it into the rice so it finishes cooking with the rice.
  11. When the rice is tender and the chicken is fully cooked, remove from heat. Let it rest and cover for 5–10 minutes.
  12. Fluff the rice gently with a fork, remove the bay leaf, and serve the jollof rice together with the chicken.

    Tips

  • Use parboiled rice for better texture.

  • You can adjust the heat by adding more pepper if you prefer a spicier jollof.

  • For a smokier flavor, try “party jollof” by allowing the rice to stick slightly to the base for a few minutes before serving.

Note: If the sauce becomes too thick before adding rice, you can dilute it slightly with warm stock or water.

Nutrition Facts (Approx.)

Per serving (rice + one piece of chicken): ~ 600 kcal, 25 g protein, 55 g carbs, 30 g fat.
